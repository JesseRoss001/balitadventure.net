Introduction to Bali's Beaches

Bali, often referred to as the "Island of the Gods," is home to an incredible array of beaches that cater to all types of travelers. From luxurious white sand beaches to tranquil volcanic shores, Bali offers diverse swimming experiences. Here's your guide to the top beaches for swimming, organized by region.

South Bali Beaches

Nusa Dua Beach

Known for its calm waters, Nusa Dua Beach is ideal for families and offers snorkeling and paddleboarding activities.

Additional Activities: Visitors can enjoy water sports like kayaking and stand-up paddleboarding. The area is also home to the Bali National Golf Club and the Bali Collection shopping complex.

Location: Nusa Dua, Badung Regency, Bali.

Geger Beach

A quieter option with serene waters and local seaweed farms adding a unique touch.

Additional Activities: Sunbathing and enjoying local cuisine at beachfront cafes.

Location: Sawangan, Nusa Dua, Badung Regency, Bali.

Jimbaran Beach

Perfect for swimming and famous for its seafood restaurants lining the shore.

Additional Activities: Dining at beachfront seafood restaurants and witnessing traditional fishing activities.

Location: Jimbaran, South Kuta, Badung Regency, Bali.

Seminyak Beach

Golden sands and moderate waves make it a vibrant beach for beginner surfers and swimmers.

Additional Activities: Surfing lessons, sunbathing, and enjoying nightlife at renowned beach clubs like Ku De Ta and Potato Head.

Location: Seminyak, Kuta, Badung Regency, Bali.

East Bali Beaches

Virgin Beach (Pasir Putih)

A secluded gem with turquoise waters, ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

Additional Activities: Snorkeling and relaxing at local warungs (small eateries) offering fresh seafood.

Location: Perasi Village, Karangasem Regency, Bali.

Candidasa Beach

A serene retreat with calm waters, perfect for a peaceful day by the sea.

Additional Activities: Exploring nearby attractions like the Tenganan Village and the Water Palace of Tirta Gangga.

Location: Candidasa, Karangasem Regency, Bali.

North Bali Beaches

Lovina Beach

Famous for its black volcanic sands and early morning dolphin tours.

Additional Activities: Dolphin watching tours, snorkeling, and hot springs nearby.

Location: Lovina, Buleleng Regency, Bali.

Pemuteran Beach

Home to coral reefs and calm waters, great for snorkeling and swimming.

Additional Activities: Snorkeling, diving, and visiting the nearby Bali Barat National Park.

Location: Pemuteran, Gerokgak, Buleleng Regency, Bali.

West Bali Beaches

Medewi Beach

Known for its long waves, Medewi is quieter and perfect for a swim close to shore.

Additional Activities: Surfing and enjoying the rural Balinese landscape.

Location: Medewi, Pekutatan, Jembrana Regency, Bali.

Southeastern Islands

Mushroom Bay (Nusa Lembongan)

Clear waters and white sands create an idyllic swimming environment.

Additional Activities: Snorkeling, diving, and exploring the island's other attractions like Devil's Tear.

Location: Nusa Lembongan, Klungkung Regency, Bali.

Crystal Bay (Nusa Penida)

True to its name, this beach features crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life.

Additional Activities: Snorkeling to see vibrant marine life and diving to spot the elusive Mola Mola (sunfish).

Location: Nusa Penida, Klungkung Regency, Bali.

Tips for Swimmers in Bali

Pay Attention to Flags

Always swim in designated safe zones to avoid rip currents.

Best Times

Swim during early mornings or late afternoons for calm waters and scenic views.

Pack Essentials

Sunscreen, water, and snacks are must-haves, especially for more remote beaches.

Respect Nature

Avoid littering and respect local customs, as some beaches are culturally significant.

Honourable Mentions

While the beaches mentioned above are the most suitable for swimming, Bali also has a wealth of other coastal gems worth exploring. Some are better for surfing, while others are perfect for sunbathing or enjoying a peaceful escape. Here are a few additional recommendations:

Padang Padang Beach

Famous for its appearance in the movie Eat, Pray, Love, Padang Padang Beach is known for its unique entrance through a hollow rock and its excellent conditions for swimming during low tide.

Kuta Beach

Although more renowned for surfing, the shallow waters close to the shore are suitable for a quick swim.

Amed Beach

With its volcanic black sand and proximity to coral reefs, Amed Beach is a great spot for snorkeling and swimming.

Diverse Marine Life

Sustainability Efforts
